Product Introduction

Purple skin garlic is recognized by the distinctive purple streaks on its outer protective skins. This variety is prized for its strong, pungent flavor, tight cloche wrapping, and excellent storage characteristics — making it the garlic of choice for culinary traditions across Africa, Southeast Asia, and South Asia.

Grown in the fertile plains of Shandong province, our purple skin garlic benefits from the region's ideal soil composition, temperate climate, and generations of farming expertise. Each bulb develops plump, uniform cloves with a high allicin content — the compound responsible for garlic's characteristic aroma and health benefits.

ExCrop controls the entire export process: from harvesting and field-curing to size sorting, quality inspection, and container loading. Our cold-storage capacity ensures consistent supply from June through March, while our proximity to Qingdao Port guarantees efficient, on-time delivery to your destination port.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about purple skin garlic

What distinguishes purple skin garlic from pure white garlic?
Purple skin garlic features outer skins with distinctive purple streaks. It is known for a more pungent, robust flavor and tighter clove wrapping compared to pure white garlic. It is particularly popular in African, Southeast Asian, and South Asian markets.
Which markets prefer purple skin garlic?
Purple skin garlic is in high demand across Africa (Nigeria, Ghana, Ivory Coast), Southeast Asia (Vietnam, Thailand, Indonesia), and South Asia. These markets value its strong flavor profile and competitive pricing.
How should purple skin garlic be stored during transit?
Fresh purple skin garlic should be transported in reefer containers maintained at -1°C to 0°C with proper ventilation. This temperature range prevents sprouting and preserves quality throughout the 15-30 day transit period.
Is purple skin garlic available year-round?
Yes. The new crop is harvested in June, and cold-storage facilities allow us to supply purple skin garlic continuously from June through March of the following year.
