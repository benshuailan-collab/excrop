Why Malaysia Imports Garlic from China

Malaysia's garlic consumption consistently exceeds 70,000 tons annually, with the vast majority sourced through imports due to the country's tropical climate, which is fundamentally unsuitable for large-scale garlic cultivation. The multi-ethnic makeup of Malaysian society, encompassing Malay, Chinese, and Indian culinary traditions, ensures strong and diverse garlic demand across all market segments, from wet markets to modern retail and food manufacturing. Chinese garlic has secured a dominant position in the Malaysian market through its combination of reliable year-round supply, competitive pricing, and the variety of grades and sizes that different distribution channels require. The geographic proximity between China and Malaysia, reflected in a short 12-15 day transit time, ensures fresh product arrival and enables agile inventory management. Shandong's mature cold storage infrastructure also supports Malaysia's festive season demand patterns, allowing importers to build inventory ahead of Chinese New Year, Ramadan, and Deepavali when consumption spikes significantly.

Garlic Import FAQ — Malaysia

What are the Malaysian food safety requirements for garlic imports?
Garlic imports into Malaysia are regulated by the Food Safety and Quality Division (FSQD) under the Ministry of Health. Importers must register and obtain a Food Import Permit. Required documentation includes a Phytosanitary Certificate, Certificate of Origin, and health certificate. Shipments are subject to inspection at Port Klang or Penang. We provide all necessary export documentation and ensure compliance with Malaysian food safety regulations, including maximum residue limits for pesticides.
Does Malaysia require an import permit specifically for garlic?
Yes, garlic imports into Malaysia require an Approved Permit (AP) issued by the Ministry of Agricultural and Food Security (MAFS). The AP system regulates the import of agricultural products to ensure quality and food safety. Importers must be registered with MAFS and obtain the AP before shipment. We work with Malaysian importers to ensure all export-side documentation supports the AP application and customs clearance process.
What is the best time to order garlic for the Malaysian market?
Malaysia's garlic demand is relatively stable year-round but sees notable increases during festive periods including Chinese New Year, Ramadan, and Deepavali. The optimal ordering window is July to September following the Chinese harvest, when fresh crop garlic is available at the best prices. For festive season supply, we recommend ordering 6-8 weeks in advance. Our cold storage facilities ensure year-round availability to meet Malaysia's consistent market demand.
