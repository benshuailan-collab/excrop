Why Brazil Imports Garlic from China

Brazil ranks among the top garlic-consuming nations in the Americas, with annual demand exceeding 250,000 tons driven by its rich culinary traditions across every region. While domestic cultivation in Goias, Santa Catarina, and Minas Gerais contributes meaningfully to supply, the harvest season from August to November leaves a substantial gap during the remaining months. Chinese garlic fills this critical void with consistent volume and reliable quality that Brazilian wholesalers and retailers have come to depend on. Shandong's agricultural expertise produces garlic with uniform bulb sizes and excellent shelf life, attributes that are particularly valued in Brazil's competitive wholesale markets. Furthermore, the established shipping routes from Qingdao to Santos provide a dependable supply chain, and Chinese garlic's competitive pricing helps Brazilian importers maintain healthy margins despite long-distance freight costs and applicable trade duties.

Garlic Import FAQ — Brazil

What import documentation is required for garlic entering Brazil?
Brazil requires a Licenciamento de Importacao (Import License) processed through the Siscomex system, issued by the Ministry of Agriculture (MAPA). Importers must also provide a Phytosanitary Certificate from the origin country, Certificate of Origin for tariff purposes, and a fumigation certificate. We supply all required export documentation to ensure smooth Siscomex registration and customs clearance at Santos or Itajai ports.
Are there anti-dumping duties on Chinese garlic imports to Brazil?
Brazil has historically applied anti-dumping measures on garlic imports from China, with duties reviewed periodically by the Brazilian trade authority (Camex). Current rates and applicability should be verified with your customs broker at the time of ordering, as trade policies may change. We work closely with Brazilian importers to provide all necessary documentation and competitive pricing to help manage overall landed cost.
What is the best season to import garlic into Brazil?
Brazil's domestic garlic harvest runs from August to November, creating an import-dependent window from December through July when local supply is insufficient. The optimal ordering period is from June to September, aligning with the Chinese fresh harvest to secure the best pricing and quality. Planning shipments to arrive ahead of the Brazilian off-season ensures maximum market advantage.
