Why Nigeria Imports Garlic from China

Nigeria, Africa's most populous nation, has seen garlic demand surge alongside rapid population growth and an expanding food processing sector. While local cultivation in Kaduna, Kano, and Sokoto states contributes to supply, domestic output remains far below national consumption needs, particularly during the dry season when northern harvests are unavailable. Chinese garlic has emerged as the primary import source for Nigerian distributors due to its unmatched combination of volume capacity, competitive pricing, and consistent quality. Shandong's established agricultural infrastructure ensures reliable year-round supply through cold storage, which is critical for a market where demand peaks from November through March. Chinese garlic also offers the bulb sizes and packaging flexibility that Nigerian wholesale and retail channels require, making it the practical choice for importers serving both local markets and West African re-export networks.

Garlic Import FAQ — Nigeria

What is Form M and why is it needed for garlic imports to Nigeria?
Form M is a mandatory import documentation required by the Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) for all imports into the country. It serves as the basis for foreign exchange allocation and customs processing. Nigerian importers must obtain Form M through their bank before initiating the garlic import process. We provide all supporting export documents including Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Phytosanitary Certificate, and Certificate of Origin to facilitate Form M approval and smooth clearance at Lagos ports.
Does Nigeria require SON certification for imported garlic?
The Standards Organisation of Nigeria (SON) oversees product quality standards. For fresh garlic, SON requires a Conformity Assessment through the SONCAP (SON Conformity Assessment Programme) for certain product categories. Fresh agricultural produce primarily requires phytosanitary certification and fumigation treatment. We recommend confirming current SONCAP applicability with your clearing agent, and we provide all necessary export-side certifications to support compliance.
What is the best time to import garlic into Nigeria?
Nigeria experiences peak garlic demand during the dry season from November to March, when local supply from northern states is limited. The optimal ordering window is July to October, following the Chinese harvest in June, to secure fresh crop garlic at competitive prices and ensure shipments arrive ahead of the high-demand dry season. Our cold storage facilities enable year-round supply to match Nigeria's market cycles.
