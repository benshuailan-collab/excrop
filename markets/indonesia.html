Why Indonesia Imports Garlic from China

Indonesia stands as one of the world's largest garlic importers, with annual consumption exceeding 500,000 tons while domestic production covers less than 5% of total demand. The tropical climate across the archipelago limits large-scale garlic cultivation, making imports essential to meet the needs of both households and the thriving food processing industry. Chinese garlic has become the preferred source for Indonesian importers due to its consistent quality, uniform bulb size, and highly competitive pricing driven by Shandong's mature agricultural supply chain. The geographic proximity between China and Indonesia also translates to shorter transit times compared to alternative origins, ensuring fresher product on arrival. Additionally, China's ability to supply year-round through cold storage facilities aligns perfectly with Indonesia's steady demand, which peaks notably during Ramadan and Eid festivities when garlic consumption surges across the nation.

Garlic Import FAQ — Indonesia

What import permit is required to bring garlic into Indonesia?
Garlic imports into Indonesia require an Import Identification Number (API) and a Horticultural Product Import Permit (IPH) issued by the Ministry of Trade. Importers must also register with the Indonesian Quarantine Agency (Barantan) for phytosanitary compliance. We provide all necessary export documents including Certificate of Origin and Phytosanitary Certificate to support customs clearance at Jakarta or Surabaya ports.
Does Indonesian customs require SNI certification for garlic?
Indonesia's SNI (National Standard of Indonesia) applies to certain processed garlic products, but fresh garlic bulbs primarily require phytosanitary certification and fumigation treatment compliant with Barantan regulations. We ensure all shipments meet Indonesian quarantine standards and provide fumigation certificates with every order.
When is the best time to order garlic for the Indonesian market?
The best purchasing window is from July to September, following the Chinese garlic harvest in June. Ordering during this period ensures access to fresh crop garlic at the most competitive prices. For year-round supply, our cold storage facilities maintain garlic quality from June through March, allowing Indonesian importers to stock up before the Ramadan and Eid demand peaks.
