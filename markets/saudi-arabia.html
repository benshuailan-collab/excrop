Why Saudi Arabia Imports Garlic from China

Saudi Arabia's arid climate and limited freshwater resources make large-scale garlic cultivation impractical, resulting in heavy reliance on imports to meet national consumption needs. Garlic is a staple ingredient in Saudi cuisine, featured prominently in everyday cooking as well as in the elaborate feasts prepared for Ramadan, Eid celebrations, and family gatherings. Chinese garlic has become the dominant import source, prized for its large bulb sizes, clean white appearance, and long shelf life that suits the GCC distribution model. Saudi buyers particularly favor pure white garlic in 6.0cm and above grades for both retail and hospitality channels. Shandong's production scale ensures the consistent volume that the Saudi market demands, while competitive pricing helps importers manage costs in a price-conscious retail environment. The established maritime route from Qingdao to Jeddah provides a dependable supply chain, and year-round cold storage availability means Saudi importers can secure inventory well ahead of peak demand periods.

Garlic Import FAQ — Saudi Arabia

What are the SFDA requirements for importing garlic into Saudi Arabia?
The Saudi Food and Drug Authority (SFDA) regulates food imports and requires that all garlic shipments comply with Saudi food safety standards. Importers must register with the SFDA and obtain a Shipment Clearance Certificate. Required documents include a Phytosanitary Certificate, Certificate of Origin, and health certificate. We provide all necessary export-side documentation and ensure shipments meet SFDA standards for pesticide residues and food safety to facilitate smooth clearance at Jeddah Islamic Port or King Abdulaziz Port in Dammam.
When does garlic demand peak in Saudi Arabia?
Saudi Arabia experiences significant garlic demand surges during Ramadan and the Eid al-Fitr period, when household cooking and food service consumption increase dramatically. Importers typically begin building inventory 2-3 months ahead of Ramadan. We recommend placing orders by December or January for Ramadan shipments, and our cold storage facilities ensure fresh garlic is available year-round to meet these seasonal peaks at competitive pricing.
What payment terms are available for Saudi Arabian importers?
We accept T/T (Telegraphic Transfer) and L/C (Letter of Credit) for all shipments to Saudi Arabia. T/T, L/C — terms negotiable based on order size and partnership duration. For established clients, we offer flexible payment arrangements. Please contact us to discuss the most suitable payment structure for your business needs.
