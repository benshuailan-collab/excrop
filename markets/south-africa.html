Why South Africa Imports Garlic from China

South Africa is the largest garlic market in southern Africa, with demand driven by both domestic consumption and re-export to neighboring countries. While South Africa has a domestic garlic industry centered in the Western Cape, Free State, and Limpopo provinces, local production covers only about 40-50% of total demand. The remainder is imported, with China being a key supplier due to its competitive pricing, reliable supply, and comprehensive product range. South African importers value Chinese garlic for its consistency in size grading, which is critical for retail packaging standards in the South African market. The food service sector, including restaurants and hospitality, also drives strong demand for premium white garlic. Additionally, the growing food manufacturing industry in South Africa has increased demand for dehydrated garlic products used in spice blends, soups, and processed foods. Durban serves as the primary port of entry, with efficient logistics infrastructure connecting to major distribution centers in Johannesburg and Cape Town.

Garlic Import FAQ — South Africa

What are the phytosanitary requirements for garlic imports into South Africa?
Garlic imports into South Africa require a valid phytosanitary certificate from the exporting country and must comply with DAFF (Department of Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries) import conditions. The garlic must be free from soil and plant debris, and may be subject to inspection upon arrival. We provide all necessary phytosanitary documentation and ensure shipments meet South African import standards. Importers should also verify whether an import permit from DAFF is required for their specific shipment.
Can you ship to Cape Town as well as Durban?
Yes. While Durban is our primary destination port for South Africa, we also ship to Cape Town for importers in the Western Cape province. Transit time to Cape Town is approximately 28-32 days. The choice of port depends on your distribution network and final delivery location. For importers based in Johannesburg, Durban is typically the most cost-effective routing. We can discuss the best option based on your specific logistics requirements.
How do you handle the Southern Hemisphere season difference for garlic supply?
China's garlic harvest is in June, while South Africa's domestic season runs from September to November. This creates an excellent complementary supply window: Chinese new crop garlic arrives in South Africa during July-September, precisely when local stock is depleted and before the domestic harvest begins. For year-round supply, our cold storage facilities maintain garlic quality from June through March of the following year. We recommend placing orders 6-8 weeks in advance during peak demand periods (especially before the festive season) to secure optimal pricing and vessel space.
