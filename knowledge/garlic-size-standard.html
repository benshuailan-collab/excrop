Garlic Size Standards and Export Specifications

Understanding garlic bulb sizing, grading systems, and how to choose the right specification for your market.

Quick Answer: Chinese export garlic is graded by bulb diameter: 4.5cm, 5.0cm, 5.5cm, 6.0cm, and 6.5cm+. Larger sizes command premium prices. Choose based on your target market and price segment.

Understanding Garlic Size Grading

Garlic size grading is the process of sorting garlic bulbs by diameter, measured at the widest point of the bulb. This standardization is critical for international trade as it ensures consistency, facilitates pricing, and meets the specific requirements of different markets. In the Chinese garlic export industry, five primary size grades are recognized and used in commercial transactions.

Size Grade Diameter Typical Use Market Positioning
4.5cm 45mm and up Bulk processing, food manufacturing Economy / Industrial
5.0cm 50mm and up Wholesale, market stalls, street vendors Standard / Mass market
5.5cm 55mm and up Retail, supermarket distribution Mid-range / Retail
6.0cm+ 60mm and up Premium retail, food service Premium
6.5cm+ 65mm and up High-end retail, gift packaging Luxury / Top tier

How Garlic is Graded

After harvest, garlic goes through a multi-step grading process. First, the bulbs are cured (dried) for 7-10 days to toughen the outer skin. Then they pass through mechanical grading machines with calibrated holes that sort bulbs by diameter. Each size grade is then visually inspected to remove damaged, diseased, or deformed bulbs. Finally, the graded garlic is packed according to buyer specifications.

Quality Criteria Beyond Size

  • Bulb firmness: should be solid to the touch, not soft or spongy
  • Skin coverage: minimum 80% of bulb should be covered by outer skin
  • Clove count: typically 8-12 cloves per bulb for standard Chinese varieties
  • Absence of mold, rot, or pest damage
  • Root trimming: roots should be cleanly cut to 5mm or less
  • Stem length: cut to 2-3cm for standard export, or fully trimmed for some markets

Pro Tip: When requesting a quote, always specify the size grade(s) you need. Mixed-size loads (e.g., 50% of 5.5cm and 50% of 6.0cm+) are possible and may offer cost savings compared to single-grade shipments.

Choosing the Right Size for Your Market

Different markets have different size preferences based on consumer expectations, retail standards, and culinary traditions. Understanding these preferences helps you make the right purchasing decision.

Market Region Preferred Sizes Reason
Southeast Asia 5.0cm - 5.5cm Balances price and quality for mass market
Middle East 5.5cm - 6.0cm+ Prefers larger bulbs for retail display
Africa 5.0cm - 6.0cm Mix of retail and wholesale needs
South America 5.5cm - 6.5cm+ Strong preference for premium large bulbs
Europe 6.0cm+ High retail standards require large, uniform bulbs

Size and Price Relationship

Garlic pricing is directly tied to size grade. As a general rule, each step up in size (e.g., from 5.0cm to 5.5cm) increases the unit price by 10-20%. The price premium for 6.5cm+ garlic can be 50% or more above 5.0cm garlic. This reflects the lower yield of larger bulbs (a smaller percentage of the harvest meets the 6.5cm+ threshold) and the higher demand from premium retail markets.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the most popular garlic size for export?
The 5.5cm grade is the most widely traded export size, offering a good balance of quality and price. It meets most retail standards while remaining affordable for mass-market distribution. The 5.0cm and 6.0cm grades are also commonly traded.
Can I order mixed sizes in one container?
Yes. Many importers order mixed-size loads, such as 50% 5.5cm and 50% 6.0cm. This can be more cost-effective than ordering a single grade and allows you to serve different customer segments. Specify your size mix when requesting a quote.
How is garlic size measured?
Garlic bulb diameter is measured at the widest point of the bulb using calibrated grading rings or mechanical sorting machines. The measurement refers to the minimum diameter for each grade. For example, a 5.5cm grade means all bulbs measure at least 55mm in diameter.
