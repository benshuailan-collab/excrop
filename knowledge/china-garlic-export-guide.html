China Garlic Export Guide

Understanding the Chinese garlic export industry, key producing regions, export data, and best practices for international buyers.

Quick Answer: China is the world's largest garlic producer and exporter, accounting for over 70% of global garlic trade. Shandong Province is the primary export hub, shipping to 150+ countries annually.

China Garlic Industry Overview

China is the world's largest garlic producer, cultivating approximately 12-15 million tons annually across roughly 800,000 hectares of farmland. Of this total, about 1.5-2.0 million tons are exported each year, making China the dominant force in the global garlic trade. The Chinese garlic industry supports millions of farmers, processors, and traders along a sophisticated supply chain that spans from planting to cold storage, processing, packaging, and export logistics.

Key Garlic Producing Regions

Garlic is cultivated across multiple provinces in China, but the primary production and export centers are concentrated in a few key regions:

Region Province Characteristics Export Share
Shandong Shandong Largest export hub, cold storage capacity, processing facilities ~60% of total exports
Henan Henan Large production volume, mid-range pricing ~15% of exports
Jiangsu Jiangsu Premium quality, early harvest ~10% of exports
Yunnan Yunnan Off-season production, purple skin varieties ~5% of exports

Why Shandong: Shandong Province dominates Chinese garlic exports due to its ideal climate, decades of farming expertise, massive cold storage infrastructure (500,000+ MT capacity), and proximity to Qingdao Port, one of China's largest container ports.

Garlic Export Season and Supply Cycle

Understanding the Chinese garlic supply cycle is essential for importers to optimize purchasing timing and pricing. The annual cycle begins with planting in October, followed by winter dormancy, spring growth, and harvest in late May to June.

Supply Timeline

  • October: Garlic planting season begins
  • March-April: Spring garlic shoots (green garlic) available
  • Late May - June: Main harvest season for fresh garlic
  • July - August: Curing, sorting, and grading of new crop
  • September - March: Cold storage garlic supply (year-round availability)

Export Process and Documentation

The Chinese garlic export process involves several regulated steps to ensure product quality and compliance with international trade standards. Exporters must be registered with China Customs and hold proper export qualifications. Every shipment undergoes phytosanitary inspection before departure.

  • Supplier registration with China Customs (export qualification)
  • Pre-export phytosanitary inspection by Customs officials
  • Issuance of Phytosanitary Certificate
  • Fumigation treatment and certification (as required by destination)
  • Certificate of Origin issuance by CCPIT or Customs
  • Container loading inspection and documentation

Quality Standards and Grading

Chinese garlic for export is graded by bulb diameter, with the most common export sizes being 4.5cm, 5.0cm, 5.5cm, 6.0cm, and 6.5cm+. Larger sizes command premium prices in most markets. Quality assessment also considers skin coverage, bulb firmness, absence of disease or mold, and uniformity within each grade.

Major Export Markets

Chinese garlic is exported to over 150 countries. The top import markets include Indonesia, Vietnam, the Philippines, Malaysia, Brazil, Nigeria, Saudi Arabia, the UAE, Egypt, and South Africa. Each market has specific requirements regarding preferred varieties, sizes, packaging, and documentation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the annual garlic export volume from China?
China exports approximately 1.5 to 2.0 million tons of garlic annually, representing over 70% of global garlic trade. The exact figure fluctuates year to year based on harvest yields, global demand, and trade policies.
Which Chinese port is the main garlic export hub?
Qingdao Port in Shandong Province is the primary garlic export hub, handling the majority of garlic shipments from China. Its proximity to the major garlic production regions in Shandong ensures efficient logistics. Other ports used include Tianjin and Lianyungang.
How has the Chinese garlic export industry evolved?
Over the past two decades, China's garlic industry has evolved from small-scale farming to a highly organized export sector with modern cold storage facilities, standardized grading systems, HACCP-certified processing lines, and global logistics networks. Quality control and traceability have significantly improved, meeting the requirements of increasingly strict international import standards.
